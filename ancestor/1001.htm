  General Notes:

[Jonathan.ftw]

Taxed in that part of Rowan Co., North Carolina which is now the southern part of Davidson County. Moses Parke taxed for 320 a and 1 WP. (this would mean that he was the only male over the age of 16 in the household.

In 1768 Moses Parks is listed on the tax list of John Ford in that area of Rowan Co., North Carolina which became Davidson Co., North Carolina. He is listed next to George Parks.

Will Book C page 9 #204. MOSES PARKS 16 Jul 1822, prb in May 1828 Ct. I give to my wf Mary Parks two feather beds and furniture, and a walnut chest and other furniture, now in the little room in my house with the fireplace, to dispose of as she sees fit. I also give my wf a horse and a fifth of my cattle, hogs, and sheep, as well as her living from my plantation during her lifetime, and at her demise I give the above mentioned stock to my son Geroge. I give to my children Thomas, John, Ollivy (Oliver?), Moses, James and Polly, L15 NC currency to be divided among them equally, I give the plantation on which I live to my son George, with the exception of my bequests to my wf, together with all of the property not specifically willed to my other children. Sole exr: Trusty friend James Cunningham, Esq. Wit. Niles Jay Robinson and Andrew Rees, Jurat. Isaac Alwexander, CMC.
