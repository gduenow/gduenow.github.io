  General Notes:

[Jonathan.ftw]

In 1759 he was listed on the Rowan Co., North Carolina tax list with George Park who was believed to be his brother. This may in fact be in error. They might be father and son and therefore not belonging to this family at all. If he was 16 to 21 at this time he would have been born 1738-1743.

Noah Parke was named on a militia list under James Smith Esqr. Edward Turner was the Sergt with Thomas Foster, John Turner, john Leaff, Isaac Holeman, John Sloan, Edward McGuire, John Ford, Jacob Likens, William Moore, Samuel Anderson, Richard Green and Josheway Whitehead.

Noah Parks was listed as a Guard at the Gaol of Salisbury, for the District of Salisbury along with Joseph Hodge, John Reed, Madad Reed, Richard Giles, William Lewis, Jacob Frick, Sandy Rindleman and Henry Fullenwider.

The area covered by the tax list is the entire northwestern quadrant of North Carolina. George and Noah Park are listed in the same household with 2 tithables. This would mean that both would be over 16 and probably over 21.

Noah Parks was listed in Capt. Berger's District with a monetary valuation of 220.4.2. No acreage or polls listed on this tax list.

In 1796 Noah Parcks was enumerated with 690 A, 1 wp, on the list of George Fisher's Company in Sourthern Rowan County area. Those of interest enumerated on this list were Ebenezer Parcks 165 A, 1wp.
