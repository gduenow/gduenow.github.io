Deed of Partition - James & Thomas Winn

Thos. Hart Commissioner
Deed of
Partition to
hold in severalty
James Winn
and
Thomas Winn

approved of 12th
Oct. 1826 dackd &
ord. recorded .

This Indenture made and entered into this the 10th day of October in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and twenty six between Thomas Hart Commissioner of the County of Clarke and State of Kentucky of the one part and James Winn and Thomas Winn of the State of Missouri of the other part, witnessed that whereas the honorable Clarke Circuit in a suit in chancery therein between James Winn Complainant against William Winn and others defendants at the September Term of said Court 1826 pronounced an interlocutory decree appointing Thomas Hart Commissioner for the purpose of conveying to the said James Winn and Thomas Winn in severalty the land in the Bill and exhibits mentioned which fell by desent to William Winn, John Winn, Thomas Winn, Daniel Winn, Rachael Faulkner, late Richard Winn, Frankey Harris late Frankey Winn, Thomas Warren, and James Winn, the legal heirs and representatives of Thomas Winn Senr died. The said Thomas Hart Commissioner as aforesaid by virtue of said decree, and in consideration of the premises on behalf of each and every one of said heirs of said Thomas Winn, Senr. decd, doth grant bargain sell alien and confirm unto the said James Winn and Thomas Winn their heirs and assigns forever in severalty two lots of land the one hereby conveyed to James Winn is situated in Clarke County lying on the waters of Hood Creek and Bounded as follows to wit:: Beginning at a stake in the intersection of the Ohio Company’s line in the middle of the public road leading from Winchester to the Gasssy Lick thence running with said Ohio Company’s line S77oE604/10 poles to a stake corner to John Higgins, thence N21oW646/10 poles to an elm corner to Sarah Johnson and Richard Halley thence with Halley’s line N23o10’W114/10 poles to two sugar trees also corner to said Halley, thence with another of his lines S 10o36’E 61/10 poles to a stake thence S25o 45’W 61 poles to the Beginning, containing twelve acres three cords and twenty square poles of land. And the lot of land hereby conveyed to the said Thomas Winn is situated in said County of Clarke lying on the waters of Hood Creek and adjoining the above described lott. And is Bounded as follows to wit: Beginning at a stake in the intersection of the Ohio Company’s line with the middle of the publics road leading from Winchester to the Grass Lick thence running N25¾o E61 poles to a stake in Richard Halley’s line and bearing S70o36’W61/10 poles from two sugar trees being his corner. Thence with said Halley’s line S70o36’W passing another of his corners and continuing the same course with Casts line 972/10 poles to a stake in the said Ohio Company’s line thence with that line S70½ E 683/10 poles to the Beginning containing twelve acres three cords and twenty square poles of land.
The said James Winn and Thomas to have and to hold the aforesaid land and premises in severalty as designated to them to their heirs and assigns forever. And the said Thomas Hart Commissioner as aforesaid will on behalf of said heirs by virtue of said decree warrant and forever defend the land and premises hereby conveyed against the claim or claims of all and every person or persons whatsoever unto the said James Winn and Thomas Winn to them their heirs and assigns forever to hold said land as hereby conveyed separately and in severalty. In Testimony whereof the said Thomas Hart as Commissioner doth hereunto set his hand and affix his seal the day and year first written above.
All Thomas Hart
Lam M. Taylor Commissioner
Geo. Smith



Kentucky
Clarke Circuit Sct
September Three 1826 - I, George Smith, Deputy for Lamuel M. Taylor, Clerk of the Circuit Court aforesaid to certify that this Deed from Thomas Hart the Commissioner therein named to James Winn and Thomas Winn was this day produced in Court, examined and approved of by the Court acknowledged by the said Commissioner to be his act and Deed hand and seal for the purposes therein named, and ordered to be recorded, which is done in said office.
Given under my hand as Deputy as aforesaid this 13th day of October 1826
Geo Smith Dep
for Lam M. Taylor C.C.C.C.
