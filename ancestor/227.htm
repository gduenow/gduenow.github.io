Thomas Winn

  General Notes:

It is only an assumption that Thomas Winn and Elizabeth are the parents of Thomas, George and Owen Winn. The Three Lives Lease for Thomas Winn and Elizabeth Winn point in that direction. There is a written account of the Winns' and Spurrs' and others' migration from Loudoun Co. VA to Fayette Co. KY in 1784 by a Spurr descendant, which is housed in the Old State Buliding of Frankfort, KY.

Loudoun County, Virginia, Deed Abstracts, 1766-1770. Ruth and Sam Sparacio
(Loudoun Co., Va., DB G:53-57) March 31, 1768. John Ashton of Washington Parish, Westmoreland County, to Thomas Winn of Cameron Parish, Loudoun County, Planter, in consideration of rents and covenants .. hath farm let parcel of land in the tenure and occupation of Thomas Winn now being containing 200 acres more or less being that plan-tation whereon Thomas Winn hath for some years past dwelt part of a larger tract of land now in possession of said Thomas Winn lying and being in Cameron Parish, Loudoun County, on branches of Difficult Run taken up and patented by Colo. Henry Ashton .. to hold during natural lives of Thomas Winn, Owen and John his Sons and longest liver of them .. paying on first day August the rent of £5 current money of Virginia ..
Wits: George Winn, Benja. Cockrill /s/ John Ashton
Daniel Atkins /s/ Thomas Winn
At a court held 14th November 1768 .. Indenture and memorandum endorsed proved .. ordered to be recorded.
