  General Notes:

In the name of God Amen - I Sarah Johnson of Howard County and Missouri being old But of perfect mind and memory thanks bee to God for his mercies calling to mind the Situation of old age Knowing that it is appointed for all boath male and female once to die Do make and ordain this my last will and Testament principally and first of all I Do commit my Soul unto the hands of almight God who gave it to me and my body to the Earth to be burreyd in a Decent Christian like manner And it hath pleased God to bless me with boath children and grandchildren and great grandchildren Knowing that I shall before a great while By Death bee Called ___them I Desire to leave them to Gods Merciful providence and Fatherly care I hope and thrust that he will guide them whilst in this world and when his appointed time Comes that he will conduct them by his Good Spirit to the mansions of Bliss which God hath provided for all thos that believe and trust in him, and as touching at what worldly Substance it hath pleased God to bless mee with I Do Leave in the following manner viz I first do leave to my Daughter Sarrah Winn ___(wife) of Thomas Winn a Note on Thomas Winn Junior their Son ___hundred Dollars payable on the 4th Day of July 1829 which is to be left in the hands of George T. Winn my agent to collect and to pay to hur when she calls on it and to Thomas Winn Ceignor (Senior?) my son in law forty five dollars that is at this time in his hand and to Sally Ann Winn Daughter of James Winn my grandsone I Do leave my Side saddle and to Nicena Ann Winn Daughter of George T. Winn my grandsone I Do leave my Bead and all my Bead Clothing also My Mingled Calico Dress and also to William Winn my grandsone Sone of Thomas Winn my sone in law Ten Dollars in cash.
I have one hundred Dollars ____(in the?) hands of George T. Winn my Agent after the Said George T. Winn furnishes a nice Tomb Stone over my Grave and has it Enclosed with Pailings I then wish him to go to the Store and get Susanna Winn his wife a nice crape dress and if thur is then anything remaining in the hands of George T. Winn I wish him to have it.
And I the Said Sarah Johnson do hereby confess and Acknowledge this and no other to bee my last will and Testament in Witness here of I the Said Sarah Johnson have here unto Set my hand and Seal This 12th Day of September in the year of our Lord 1828.

I the Said Sarah Johnson Do publish prnounce and Declare no other to bee my last will and Testament

Witness In the Presence of hur and in the presence hur
John Heard Of Each other we have here unto Set our Sarah Johnson
George Heard Names the day and year above written mark

Above is a written abstract done by Beulah Olive Winn Brenneman, Salmon, Idaho, January 15, 1959 - "Blanks will indicate torn places in the will, the paper being brittle and fragile."

Received from Myles Johnson <mylesj@his.com> from the George Johnson file - Dr. W. Wayne Smith Collection, Rowan Co. Public Library, Salisbury NC.


Sarah married George Johnston, son of William Jr. Johnston and Sarah, on 5 Jun 1770. (George Johnston was born about 1750 in Culpeper Co. VA and died in Feb 1836 in Randolph Co. MO.)
