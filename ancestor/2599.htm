  General Notes:

From "History of Boone County" by William F. Switzler, 1882 reprint, pages 608 and 609
"Thomas G. Sims was born near New Castle, Henry county, Kentucky, July 20, 1823. He is the son of Thomas A. and Elizabeth (Morris) Sims, natives of Virginia. The subject of this sketch came to Boone county in 1836, and resided here ever since. He has devoted his time to farming and bricklaying. Was married, Decenber 17, 1853, to Miss Margaret A., daughter of James M. Hicks. They have eight children living, and one dead. Their names are Thomas A., Jeannette D., James M., Lizzie J., Lenora, Walter B., Flora T., Elmer T., and Betta. The last named is dead. Mr. Sims is a member of the Masonic order, also a memeber of the orders of United Workmen and Knights of Honor. He is an officer in the two first named lodges. Was imprisoned by the Union authorities during the war on account of his Southern politics. Mr Sims is a self-educated man, having never attended school but three months in his life. He was appointed a justice of the peace during the war. He is an excellent workman. He superintended the erection of Hardin College and the opera house at Mexico, Missouri, and assisted in building the addition to the Christian College, Columbia. He is a good-natured, jovial man, and is universally esteemed by all who know him. Mrs. Sims is a member of the Christian church."

(From V. Darcey Slaughter - below)
Death of Thos. G. Sims.
Thos. G. Sims, for many years a citizen of Boone
county, died at his home in Sturgeon Saturday morning,
January 7, 1899, of la grippe.
Mr. Sims was the son of Thomas A. and Elizabeth
Sims (nee Morris) and was born in Clark county,
Kentucky, July 20, 1833, being, therefore, 65 years, 5
months and 17 days old at the time of his death. He
came to Missouri with his parents at the age of three
years and settled in old Middletonw, Boone county. He
went to school only three months of his life, and that
was to Wm. Schooling, in an old long house still
standing in the yard of Jabex Sublett, four miles south
of Sturgeon. He was married December 17, 1853, to Miss
Margaret A. Hicks. Soon after their marriage they moved
to Bates county, Missouri, where they resided for about
three years.
Returning to Boone county, Mr. Sims engaged in
laying brick, his favorite occupation. He was appointed
justice of the peace in 1862, serving two years. At the
outbreak of the war in 1861 he cast his fortunes with
the south and was at the Fulton and Florida fights. He
lived in Columbia for two years n the early 70's; moved
to Sturgeon in 1880, where he resided until his death.
He was elected constable of Bourbon township in 1882;
appointed marshal of the Sturgeon court of common pleas
by Gof. Crittenden in 1884 and served 12 years; served
as city marshal and member of the city council of
Sturgeon at various times. He resided at Columbia in
1870-71, assisted in building Christina and Baptist
colleges, the M.E. Church, sSouth.
Mr. Sims united with the Methodist church in
Columbia in 1871.
Decease leaves a widow and ten children (five boys
and five girls), one other childr having died in
infancy. He was buried in Locust Grove Missionary
Baptist Church Cemetery, Boone Co., Missouri.42 Named
Thomas for his father - and Guthrie for his maternal
grandfather, Guthrie Morris. GUTHRIE had been the
grandfather's mother's maiden name -- Elizabeth Guthrie.
