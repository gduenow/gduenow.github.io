The Will of Thomas Turner as transcribed by V. Darcey Slaughter follows: The will of Thomas Turner is recorded in Madison Co., Kentucky. Will Book C page 254-255. In the name of God Amen I Thomas Turner of the county of Madison and State of Kentucky do make ordain and declare this instrument to be my last will and Testament revoking all others by me made. First all my just debts to be punctually paid second I give to my two youngest Sons Thomas and Benjamin my tract of land on which I now live to be equally divided between them at my decease 3rd i give to my Son Thomas Turner my negro Boy Frank and one feather Bed and furniture and that he should keep the horses and saddle and bridle that he now claims. Also I give to my daughter Hannah Turner my negro girl Betsy a Cow and calf & feather bed and furniture a side saddle and bridle to be worth Sixteen dollars Also, I give to my Daughter Nancy Turner ny negro girl Riah, a feather bed and furniture cow and calf & side saddle and bridle to be worth sixteen dollars- Also I give to my Son Benjamin Turner my negro Boy Solomon, one horse saddle and bridle to be worth one hundred dollars and my feather bed and furniture, and that he shall be educated out of my Estate, to read, write and cypher as far as the double rule of three, and also to be comfortably cloathed and boarded while getting his education. The rest residue and remainder of my Estate both real and personal tis my wish and desire for it to sold, and divided equally among all my children viz James Turner, Rachel Barnes, Edward Turner, Rebecah Howard, Sarah Martin, Ann (Smith Turner, Wm. Turner, Catharine Morgan, Thomas Turner) Martin, Elenor Johnson, John Turner, Hannah Turner, Nancy Turner and Benjamin Turner, to them and their heirs forever but should either of the four last named children die before they arrive to lawful age or have an issue their portion that is left to them that may decease to be sold and equally divided between all my children. Lastly I constitute and appoint my Son Edward Turner, and my brother Philip Turner Executors of this my last will and Testament. In Witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and seal this 12th day of October, one thousand eight hundred and eleven in presence of Gabl Ragsdale Thomas Turner John Turner James Turner NB Whereas some of the within witnesses are dead and one at a considerable distance I therefore have again this day acknowledged the within to be my last will and Testament May the eighteenth in the year Lord Eighteen hundred and fifteen Thomas Turner Sealed in presence of Thomas Jerman Jr Jesse Turner Enoch Turner Kentucky Madison County Seal

I David Irvine Clerk of the Court of the County aforesaid do hereby certify that at a county court held for Madison County on Monday the 1st day of July 1822, the foregoing Will was produced in open court and ordered to be Recorded and the same has been done accordingly

Attest David Irvine C.M.C.C.
