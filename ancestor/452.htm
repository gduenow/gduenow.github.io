  General Notes:

[Jonathan.ftw]

In 1778 Ebenezer Park was listed in Capt. Cox's District of Rowan Co., North Carolina. Those of interest enumerated on Capt. Cox's District list at this time were George Park, Tymothy Park, Nathan Parks, Allen Park, John Ellis, Charles Park, and Moses Parks.

Ebenezer Parke was enumerated as 1 wp with 287 A on the 1784 Tax list of Cap Runnions District which was in the southern Davidson County area. Others of interested enumerated on this list were Timotny Parke 242 A, 1 wp, Allin Parke, 1 wp, Charles Parke 400A, 1 wp, and Moses Parkes 320 A, 1 wp.

Ebenezar Parke Senr was listed on the tax list of Capt. Davis of Bald Mountain District along with Allen Parke, Timothy Parke, and John Parke. He was taxed for 450 a and 1 WP.

In 1796 Ebenezer Parcks was enumerated with 165 A, 1 wp, on the list of George Fisher's Company in Sourthern Rowan County area. Those of interest enumerated on this list were Noah Parcks 690 A, 1wp.

In 1800 Ebenezer Parks was enumerated on the county wide tax list for Rowan Co., North Carolina.

In 1779, Ebenezer Park was a witness to a deed record for Allen Parke. Rowan Co., North Carolina Deed Book 9 page 105, 25 Dec. 1779, Allen Park & wf Elizabeth (+) to Phineas Bunyon for 200 pounds NC money, 240 A on Cabbin Crk bought from George Smith Jr. of Pennsylvania 21 Feb 1767. Jonathan Davis, Ebenezer Parke. Feb 1780.

In 1786 Ebenezer Parks/Parker received a state grant of 270 A on the northwest side of Cabin Creek 202.p 138 25 Oct 1786. State Grant #1208 @ 50 sh per 100 A to Ebenezer Parks/Parker, 270 A on NW side of Cabin Crk.

In 1796, Ebenezer Parks sold his land in Rowan co., North Carolina. This appears to be about the time he moved to Madison Co., Kentucky (later Estill Co., Kentucky). 3219. p 820. 4 Aug 1796. Ebenezer Parks to Frederick Smith for 200 pounds, 270A on NW side of Cabin Crk. Wit: William Ward, John Rudolph. Prvd by Ward at Feb Ct 1797. 3220. p 822. 7 aug 1796. Frederick (O) Smith to Andrew Rudolph for 100 pounds hard money, 150 A on Cabin Crk; part of land conveyed by Ebenezer Parks to this Grantor on 4 Aug 1796 [14:820], Wit: William Ward, John Rudolph. Prvd by Ward at Feb Ct 1797. 3221 p. 823. 3 Aug 1796. Ebenezer Parks to Frederick Smith for 100 pounds hard money, 180 A on Cabin Crk adj this Grantor; which was a State grant to this Grantor of 7 Jul 1794. Wit: William Ward, John Rudolph. Prvd by Ward at Feb Ct 1797.
In 1794 there is reference to land owned by Allen Park being adjacent to land owned by Ebenezer Park. 171. p. 250. 27 (?) Jul 1794. State grant #2347 @ 50 sh per 100 A to Allen Park, 300 A on cabin Crk adj Ebenezer Parks. Then in Sept of 1797 Allen Parke sold this land to Clement Lannier. This is about the time he appears in Madison Co., Kentucky. 286. page 412. 5 Sep 1797 Allen Parke to Clement Lannier for 100 pounds, 300 A on Cabin Crk adj Ebenezer Park. Witn. William Cole, Michael Beebe. Prvd by Cole at Nov Ct. 1797.


On 25 Jul 1778 is record of a land grant for Ebenezer Park in Rowan Co., North Carolina. #1108 EBENEZER PARK 300 A between the mouths of two brances on NW side of Cabbin Crk being the division between Richard Quick and sd Park, the upper branch dividing Charles Park, and the lower branch dividing Jonathan Jones and him (11:138)

On 3 Feb 1779, Timothy Parkes received a land grant of 320 A bounding land owned by Ebenezer Park's. #2025, TIMOTHY PARKES 320 A., on Dry Branch of Cabbin Crk & bounding on Ebenezer Park's entry for Compliment.

In 1785 Ebenezer Park received a second land grant on Cabbin Creek. #2882 EBENEZER Parks 100 A on Dry Branch on N side of Cabbin Crk adj his own line and Jonathan Jones' conditional line.

Ebenezar Park relinquished qualifying as executor of the estate of Richard Pearson. The will of Richard Pearson was proved by Chas. Park. Phinehas Runyan qualified as Exr. Book 5 page 527, 3 Nov 1789.
