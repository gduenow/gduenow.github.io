William Parks 2,13

  • Born: Abt 1803, Madison Co., Kentucky 2,87,88,89
  • Died: Between 1850 and 1860, Saline Co., Missouri, about age 47 2

  General Notes:

[Jonathan.ftw]

He is named as a son of Jonathan Park in the Park Book outline. I have tried to verify his family with other records.

According to several Park researchers, William Park died at the Alamo. According to the listing of those who died at the Alamo there was a man named William Park killed but he was from North Carolina. William Park, son of Jonathan Park would have been listed as being from Missouri as that was where he lived at the time. At the very least he might have been listed as being from Kentucky as that is the place of his birth. William Park, son of Jonathan Park, can be verified as living in Missouri after the time of the Alamo. According to the 1830 Boone Co., Missouri census, William Park was living next door to his father-in-law John Lefler. In 1845 John Lefler died and his probate was settled in Boone Co., Missouri. Boone Co., Missouri Probate Packet 635 administrator John Gorshey granted 21 October 1845 with heirs Mary Lefler, widow; Susan Park of Howard Co., Missouri, Joseph Lefler of Boone Co., Missouri. Peggy Coy of Saline Co., Missouri, John Lefler of Saline Co., Missouri, Jacob Lefler of Boone Co., Missouri and D. Lefler of Tennessee. In 1840 William Park was living in Saline Co., Missouri enumerated next door to his brother-in-law, John Lefler. In 1850 William Park (with wife Susan) was enumerated in household #845 and his brother-in-law John Lefler was enumerated in househole #842. William Lefler disappears from the Saline Co., Missouri census between 1850 and 1860 and it is assumed that he died.

He was on the 1850 census with his wife and family but in 1860 his wife was enumerated as the head of household. It is assumed that he died between these dates.
