Allen Park 2,128

  General Notes:

[Jonathan.ftw]

He was named as a brother of Ebenezer Park in the Gum book.

He was at least 16 when part of a scouting party in 1759 in Rowan Co., North Carolina but probably 21 which would make him born ca 1738. He was 21 before 1762 as assigned land and had to be at least 21 to do so in Virginia which makes him born before 1741.

Madison Co., KY Court Order Book C page 329, December 2nd 1805, "On the Motion of Elizabeth and Amos Parks, a Certificate granted them for obtaining letters of administration on the estate of Allen Parks Deceased, on their entering into Bond whereupon they together with Absalom & Richard Giles, their Securities, entered into and acknowledged their Bond in the penalty of 130 Pounds conditioned as the law direct."

Inventory of the estate of Allen Parks, deceased. 4 Nov 1805 returned and ordered to be recorded. page 325-326.

On the 15th of May 1759, Allen Parks with Roger Turner, Joseph Bryan, George Parks, Gabriel Enochs, Peter Cross, Henry Hagy, Mirack Davis, Phillip Davis, Thomas Evans, James Whitsitt, Conrod Carn, Danl Holyfield, John Fry, Leonard Hazzard and Thomas Felps were "Private Men in a scounting party with Aventon Phelps, Willis Ellis Senr, Phillip Howard Ens and Israel Cos Sergt.

Taxed in Capt Cox's District with 1009 monetary value. No acreage of land or polls taxed are given in this listing.

Taxed in that part of Rowan Co., North Carolina which is now the southern part of Davidson County. Allin Parke taxed for 1 WP and no land. (this would mean that he was the only male over the age of 16 in the household.

Allen Parke was listed on the tax list of Capt. Davis of Bald Mountain District along with Timotny Parke, Ebenezar Parke Senr, and John Parke. He was taxed for 300 acres of land and 1 WP.

Deed Book 6 page 417 "21 February 1767 George Smith of Montgomery Township, Philadelphia Co., Pennsylvania to Allen Park for 60 pounds, 240 acres on Cabin Creek. John Davis, Israel Cox. Prvd Apr. Court 1767."

Allen Park was also a witness to the purchase of 256 acres by Israel Cox from George Smith as above and a witness to the purchase of 256 acres by John Davis from George Smith as above.

Deed Book 9 page 105 "25 December 1779 Allen Parke and wife Elizabeth to Phineas Runyon for 200 pounds North Carolina money, 240 acres on Cabbin Creek bought from George Smith Junior of Pennsylvania 21 February 1767, Jonathan Davis, Ebenezer Parke. February 1780."

Deed Book 10 page 145, "4 November 1784 State Grant #794 @50 sh the 100 acres to Charles Parke, 400 acres on both sides Cabbin Creek adj. Phineas Runyon and Allen Park."

Also referenced as #1214 Charles Parke 400 acres on Cabbin Crk both sides, adj Allin Parke's deeded land and his own Improovment.

"#3053, Allin Park 150 A on S side of Lick Crk and on end of Grassy Ridge on Dry Branch of sd crk. etc."

171. p 250 27 Jul 1794. State grant #2347 @50 sh per 100 acres to Allen Park, 300 A on Cabin Crk adj Ebenezer Parks.

286 p 412." 5 Sep 1797. Allen Park to Clement Lannier for 100 pounds, 300 acres on Cabin Crk adj Ebenezer Park, Wit: William Cole, Michael Beebe. Prvd by Cole at Nov Ct 1797."

in 1782 Allen Park was a witness to the will of George Parke along with Moses Park and Phins. Runyon.

In 1762 Allen Parks assigned a tract of land to James Smith who assigned it to Bryan Bruin.

"BRYAN BRUIN, assignee (in 1764) of James Smith, assignee (in 1764) of Allen Parks: 3 May 1762 - 7 May 1762; 222 a. on Dillon's Run on headof Thomas Edward mill run, a br. of Cacapehon; in wart - between his (Allen's) brother John Parks & in surv. adj. his (Allen's) father's last surv. of 182 a. The assignment show adj. John Parks, CC - John Parks & John Roussau. surv. John Mauzy."
