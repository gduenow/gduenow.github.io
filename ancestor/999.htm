  General Notes:

[Jonathan.ftw]

Based on being over 21 for tax list in 1759 and serving in militia in 1759. Probably older.

On the 15th of May 1759, George Parks with Roger Turner, Joseph Bryan, Allen Parks, Gabriel Enochs, Peter Cross, Henry Hagy, Mirack Davis, Phillip Davis, Thomas Evans, James Whitsitt, Conrod Carn, Danl Holyfield, John Fry, Leonard Hazzard and Thomas Felps were "Private Men" in a scouting party with Aventon Phelps, Willis Ellis Senr, Phillip Howard Ens and Israel Cox Sergt.

The area covered by the tax list is the entire northwestern quadrant of North Carolina. George and Noah Park are listed in the same household with 2 tithables. This would mean that both would be over 16 and probably over 21.

In 1778 George Park was listed in Capt. Cox's District of Rowan Co., North Carolina. Those of interest enumerated on Capt. Cox's District list at this time were Ebenezer Park, Tymothy Park, Nathan Parks, Allen Park, John Ellis, Charles Park, and Moses Parks.

In 1768 George Parks is listed on the tax list of John Ford in that area of Rowan Co., North Carolina which became Davidson Co., North Carolina. He is listed next to Moses Parks.

"The will of George (X) Parke, dated 13 Sept. 1782, probated 5 Nov. 1782, recorded Rowan County Will Book B:165, identiries him as being of Bald Mountain District. The widow Agnes was left with seven children under the age of fourteen. On 4 May 1784, William Coles, Esq. was appointed guardian of John, Mary, Agness, Joshua, Huldy, and Jacob Niclis Park, orphans of George Park decd & have 400 pound bond with James Mccay security. see Rowan County Minutes of the Court of Pleas & Quarter Sessions, Book 4:412. The Rowan County marriage bond of George Parke to Abness Nichols is dated 5 Feb. 1782 with Jacob Nichols bondsman."
